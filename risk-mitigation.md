## 🔴 HIGH-PRIORITY RISKS

### 1. CLIENT CHURN (Catastrophic Impact)

**Scenario:** Client terminates contract at Month 3 or 6  
**Financial Impact:** Revenue drops from RM 25k → RM 0 overnight  
**Probability:** Medium (30-40% for new client relationships)

#### Early Warning Signs
- Client response time slows (from hours to days)
- Feedback becomes vague or inconsistent
- Decision-maker changes or leaves company
- Payment delays without explanation
- Competitor mentioned in conversations

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Diversify to 3+ clients by M9** | Months 7-9 | RM 2k/month marketing | You |
| **Build 3-month cash buffer** | Months 1-6 | Opportunity cost | You |
| **Quarterly business reviews with client** | Every 3 months | Time only | PM |
| **Get testimonials + case studies** | Months 3, 6, 9 | Time only | PM |
| **Sign 6-month minimum contract** | Month 1 (retroactive if possible) | Legal fees RM 1k | You |

#### Contingency Plan (If Client Leaves)
1. **Immediate:** Freeze all hiring, cut discretionary spending
2. **Week 1:** Activate freelance network (offer 20% discount for quick projects)
3. **Week 2-4:** Aggressive outreach to 50 prospects (LinkedIn, referrals, past contacts)
4. **Month 2:** Pivot to project-based work if retainers don't materialize
5. **Month 3:** Consider acquisition or partnership if runway < 2 months

**Runway After Churn:**
- Month 3 churn: RM 44k cash ÷ RM 8.5k/month burn = **5.2 months to pivot**
- Month 6 churn: RM 86.5k cash ÷ RM 11.5k/month burn = **7.5 months to pivot**
- Month 9 churn: RM 138k cash ÷ RM 16k/month burn = **8.6 months to pivot**

### 2. STAFF TURNOVER (Moderate Impact)

**Scenario:** Key employee quits at Month 4 (after training, before replacement ready)  
**Financial Impact:** RM 5-10k recruitment + training cost + productivity loss  
**Probability:** High (50-60% in first year for agency roles)

#### Early Warning Signs
- Employee disengaged (misses deadlines, quiet in meetings)
- Taking more time off than usual
- Updating LinkedIn profile
- Asking about "other opportunities" or "career growth"
- Competitor poaching (common in KL media scene)

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Pay 10% above market rate** | From Day 1 | RM 1,000-1,500/month | You |
| **Quarterly performance bonuses** | Months 3, 6, 9, 12 | RM 500-1,000/quarter | You |
| **Clear career progression path** | Month 1 | Time only | You |
| **Document all workflows (SOPs)** | Months 1-3 | Time only | PM |
| **Cross-train team members** | Ongoing | Time only | PM |
| **Build freelance backup roster** | Months 2-4 | RM 500 testing cost | PM |

#### Contingency Plan (If Employee Quits)
1. **Same day:** Activate freelance backup (pre-vetted, ready to start within 48 hours)
2. **Week 1:** Post job ad on LinkedIn, JobStreet, Facebook groups
3. **Week 2:** Interview 5-10 candidates, give practical test to top 3
4. **Week 3:** Hire replacement, 2-week overlap with freelancer
5. **Month 2:** Full handover complete, freelancer released

**Critical Roles to Protect:**
- **Video Editor #1** (hardest to replace, longest ramp-up)
- **Project Manager** (client relationships at risk)

**Less Critical:**
- **Content Creator** (easier to find, you can temporarily fill role)

## 🟠 MEDIUM-PRIORITY RISKS

### 3. CASH FLOW GAP (High Impact)

**Scenario:** Client pays Net-60 instead of Net-15; you can't cover payroll  
**Financial Impact:** Missed payroll = legal issues + morale collapse  
**Probability:** Medium (40% for B2B contracts in Malaysia)

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **50% upfront payment terms** | Month 1 contract | Negotiation | You |
| **Net-15 payment terms (not Net-30/60)** | Month 1 contract | Negotiation | You |
| **Late payment penalty (1.5%/month)** | Month 1 contract | Legal review RM 500 | You |
| **Invoice immediately on delivery** | Ongoing | Time only | PM |
| **Follow up at Day 7, 14, 21** | Ongoing | Time only | PM |
| **Maintain 3-month cash buffer** | Months 1-6 | Opportunity cost | You |

#### Red Flags
- Client asks to extend payment terms after signing
- Invoices "lost" or "under review" repeatedly
- New accounts payable contact (turnover at client)
- Client experiencing their own cash issues (news, rumors)

#### Contingency Plan
1. **Day 15 overdue:** Friendly reminder email from PM
2. **Day 30 overdue:** You call client decision-maker directly
3. **Day 45 overdue:** Pause production until payment received
4. **Day 60 overdue:** Formal demand letter (lawyer, RM 500)
5. **Day 75 overdue:** Small claims court or write off

### 4. QUALITY DRIFT (High Impact)

**Scenario:** Video quality declines at Month 6; client notices and threatens to leave  
**Financial Impact:** Contract renegotiation (-20% revenue) or churn  
**Probability:** Medium (35% as volume increases)

#### Early Warning Signs
- Client feedback mentions "not as good as before"
- Revision requests increase (from 1.2 → 2.5 rounds/video)
- Team rushing to hit deadlines (corner-cutting)
- New hires not fully trained on quality standards

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Weekly QA review (random 10% sample)** | From Month 1 | 2 hrs/week | PM |
| **Client satisfaction survey (monthly)** | From Month 2 | Time only | PM |
| **Quality checklist for every video** | Month 1 | Time only (create once) | You |
| **Revision tracking (target <1.5 rounds/video)** | Ongoing | Time only | PM |
| **Monthly team training session** | From Month 3 | 2 hrs/month | You |

#### Quality Checklist (Per Video)
- [ ] Audio clear, no background noise
- [ ] Lighting consistent, no harsh shadows
- [ ] Framing correct (rule of thirds, headroom)
- [ ] Captions accurate, synced properly
- [ ] Branding (logo, colors, fonts) consistent
- [ ] Transitions smooth, not jarring
- [ ] Export format correct (platform-specific)
- [ ] File naming convention followed

### 5. BURNOUT (YOU) (High Impact)

**Scenario:** You're working 60 hrs/week at Month 5; health/relationships suffer  
**Financial Impact:** Poor decisions, missed opportunities, potential exit  
**Probability:** High (70% for founder-operators in Year 1)

#### Early Warning Signs
- Working weekends regularly
- Skipping meals or exercise
- Irritable with team or client
- Procrastinating on strategic work
- Sleep issues, anxiety, physical symptoms

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Hire PM by Month 4 (non-negotiable)** | Month 4 | RM 2,000/month | You |
| **Set hard boundaries (no work after 7 PM)** | Month 1 | Discipline | You |
| **Delegate 1 task/week to team** | Ongoing | Time only | You |
| **Take 1 full day off/week (no exceptions)** | Month 1 | Time only | You |
| **1-week vacation every quarter** | Months 3, 6, 9, 12 | RM 2-3k/trip | You |
| **Weekly CEO time block (strategy only)** | From Month 2 | 4 hrs/week | You |

#### Delegation Ladder

| Month | You Do | Delegate To |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| M1-2 | Everything | Editor (editing only) |
| M3-4 | Client comms, scripting, filming | Creator (scripting + filming) |
| M5-6 | Client comms, QA | PM (comms + QA) |
| M7-9 | Strategy, biz dev | PM (ops), Editor (quality) |
| M10-12 | Strategy only | Team (everything else) |

**Target:** You work <15 hrs/week by Month 10

### 6. SCOPE CREEP (Moderate Impact)

**Scenario:** Client asks for "small changes" that add 20 hrs/month unpaid work  
**Financial Impact:** Effective rate drops from RM 278/video → RM 200/video  
**Probability:** High (65% without clear contract terms)

#### Early Warning Signs
- Client requests "just one more thing" regularly
- Revision rounds exceed contract limit (2 rounds)
- New platforms added without price adjustment
- Turnaround time expectations shrink (48 hrs → 24 hrs)

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Clear contract: 2 revision rounds max** | Month 1 | Legal review RM 500 | You |
| **Change order process (written approval)** | Month 1 | Time only (template) | PM |
| **Track all revision requests** | Ongoing | Time only | PM |
| **Monthly scope report to client** | From Month 2 | Time only | PM |
| **Price list for add-ons** | Month 1 | Time only | You |

#### Add-On Pricing (Example)

| Service | Price |
|---------|-------|
| Extra revision round | RM 150/video |
| Rush delivery (<24 hrs) | +50% surcharge |
| Additional platform format | RM 50/video |
| Scriptwriting (if not included) | RM 100/script |
| Thumbnail design | RM 30/thumbnail |
| Analytics report | RM 500/month |

### 7. EQUIPMENT FAILURE (Moderate Impact)

**Scenario:** Camera or editing workstation dies at Month 5; production halts  
**Financial Impact:** RM 3-5k replacement + 2-3 days downtime  
**Probability:** Low (15-20% per year for electronics)

#### Mitigation Strategies

| Action | Timeline | Cost | Owner |
|--------|----------|------|-------|
| **Backup camera body (Month 2)** | Month 2 | RM 2,000 | You |
| **Spare editing workstation** | Month 6 | RM 2,500 | You |
| **Cloud backup of all footage** | Ongoing | RM 100/month | PM |
| **Equipment insurance** | Month 1 | RM 500/year | You |
| **Local repair shop relationship** | Month 1 | Time only | PM |

#### Contingency Plan
1. **Same day:** Switch to backup equipment
2. **Day 2:** Send broken gear for repair (get quote)
3. **Day 3:** Decide repair vs. replace based on quote
4. **Week 1:** Replacement purchased if repair >50% of new cost
5. **Ongoing:** Maintain backup gear for next failure
